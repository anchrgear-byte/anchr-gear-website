# Feature & Review Analysis: Premium Fitness Gloves

## Lululemon (Wunder Train / License to Train) - ~$38-42
*   **Key Features:**
    *   "Technical" fabrics (sweat-wicking, quick-drying).
    *   Perforated palms for ventilation.
    *   Silicone grip inside to prevent slipping.
    *   Minimalist aesthetic.
*   **Customer Feedback:**
    *   **Pros:** "Cute," "Comfy," "Feels like a second skin."
    *   **Cons:** "Not made for heavy weights" (padding is too thin), durability issues (seams ripping), "bad reviews for functionality."
*   **Takeaway:** People buy these for the *brand* and the *look*, not for serious heavy lifting. They are "lifestyle" gloves.

## Gymshark (Legacy Lifting Gloves) - ~$26-44
*   **Key Features:**
    *   Wrist support (often integrated wraps).
    *   Padding on palms.
    *   Bold branding.
*   **Customer Feedback:**
    *   **Pros:** "Fit perfect," "Received compliments," "Good quality."
    *   **Cons:** "Hands still hurt" (padding might be insufficient for some), "doesn't protect so much."
*   **Takeaway:** A middle ground between fashion and function. The "Legacy" line commands a higher price because of the added wrist support and "pro" look.

## G-Loves - ~$45-60
*   **Key Features:**
    *   Neoprene material (breathable, washable).
    *   "No-slip grip" with specific texture.
    *   Highly stylized designs (prints, colors).
    *   "Made in USA" angle.
*   **Customer Feedback:**
    *   **Pros:** "Best workout gloves ever," "Contour to hands," "Superior traction."
    *   **Cons:** Price is high.
*   **Takeaway:** Customers pay a premium for *specialized fit* (made for women's hands) and *unique designs* that stand out.
